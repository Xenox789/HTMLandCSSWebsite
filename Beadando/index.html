History

Formula One(more commonly known as Formula 1 or F1) is the highest class of international racing for open-wheel single-seater formula racing cars. Formula One originated from the European Motor Racing Championships of the 1920s and 1930s. The first world championship race took place at the Silverstone Circuit in the United Kingdom on 13 May 1950.

A Formula One Grand Prix event spans a weekend. It typically begins with two free practice sessions on Friday, and one free practice on Saturday. A qualifying session is held after the last free practice session. This session determines the starting order for the race on Sunday. In an effort to add even more excitement to the race weekend, the FIA introduced sprint races. These races are shorter than the traditional Grand Prix, and take place on Saturday instead of Sunday. The results then determine the starting grid for the actual Grand Prix on Sunday.
